Those Without Revised

Those “WITHOUT”

(Colossians 4:2-6) [KJV]

INTRODUCTON: THESIS: Faithful Christian’s attitude and actions toward the lost

I. Those “Without” are those:

A. Outside the Kingdom of God (Colossians 1:12-14; Ephesians 2:11-22)

1. Those under the power/control of darkness and sin

2. They are in the world

* “Without” Christ, outside of Christ’s body/church/kingdom

B. NOT a part of Christ’s Body (Acts 2:47; Ephesians 1:20-23; Ephesians 5:23)

  1. The saved are those added to the body/church/kingdom by God

II. Christians are saved to save Others (Matthew 5:13-16; 28:16-20) 

DISCUSSION:

I. The POVERTY of those without

A. All spiritual blessings are IN Christ (Ephesians 1:3)

  1. Salvation from sin (Colossians 1:12-14)
  2. Reconciliation to God (2 Corinthians 5:17-21)
  3. Adoption INTO God’s Family (John 1:12; 1 John 3:1-2) 
  4. Hope of Heaven (Colossians 1:5, 23)

B. People WITHOUT are denied these blessings (Ephesians 2:11-12) {see I.A. above}

  1. Those WITHOUT travel a road (way of life) to destruction (Matthew 7:12-14)
  2. Do WE really believe this?

II. The RESPONSIBILITY of those WITHIN toward those WITHOUT

  1. NOT sitting in Judgement (1 Corinthians 5:9-13)

1. We are to Practice, Proclaim, and Persuade

(1 John 1:5-10; 2 Timothy 4; Acts 18:1-4; 19:8; 2 Corinthians 5:11))

  1. “Walk honestly” (1 Thessalonians 4:11-12; 1 John 1:6)

1. Cannot preach Christ while being dishonest with the world without being lost

  1. “Walk in wisdom” “Walk to order one’s behavior” (Wuest)

1. It is wise to try to win the souls of those WITHOUT (Proverbs 11:30; Daniel 12:2-3)

D. “Redeem the Time” “To make wise use of every opportunity for doing good” (Wuest)

1. As we have opportunity to do good (Galatians 6:9-10)

  1. The Christian must look for opportunities to teach (Acts 8:1,4)

E. Speak graciously to them (Colossians 4:5-6; 2Timothy 2:24-26) 

  1. The speech of the Christian should be edifying (Ephesians 4:29)

a. Would gossip edify?

b. Would Teaching a Bible Class in the home of one “without” edify?

c. Would harsh, unkind words edify?

d. Would instruction about salvation, worship, and Christian living edify?

F. “Answer them”

1. There are people without who can be taught

2. Some sincerely seek an answer to the reason for our hope (1 Peter 3:15)

CONCLUSION:

I. Christians have an obligation to people outside the kingdom/church

*Christians are obligated by the great commission to teach

II. WE (Christians/church) must:

A. Abound in the LORD’s WORK (1 Corinthians 15:58)

B. “Use up” our lives for Christ (Philippians 1:21-30)

III. Walking “in” Christ, lest we find ourselves ‘without” (Revelation 22:14-17)
