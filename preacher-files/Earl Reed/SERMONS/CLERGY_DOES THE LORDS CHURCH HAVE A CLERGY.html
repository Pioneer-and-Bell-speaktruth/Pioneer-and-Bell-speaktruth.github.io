DOES THE LORD’S CHURCH HAVE A CLERGY?

(1 Peter 2:5-10)

I. Most “churches” have people known as the “clergy”

  1. The members are “laity” and the leaders are the “clergy”
  2. What does the word “clergy” mean?
  3. A Brief Word Study

Clergy A group ordained to perform pastoral or sacerdotal functions in a Christian church.

[NOTE: Pastoral and Sacerdotal]

Clerisy Intelligentsia…A class or group of persons having or claiming special enlightenment in

views or principles: the intellectuals

↓

Pastoral Of or relating to spiritual care or guidance especially of a congregation. Of or relating

to the pastor of a church.

↓

Pastor A spiritual overseer; Especially a clergyman serving a local church or parish.

[NOTE: The word “pastor” comes from the same word for “bishop” in the New Testament. To scripturally be

a “pastor” is to be a bishop, elder, or overseer. All words denote the same office In the Lord’s church.]

↓

Sacerdotal Of or relating to priests or a priesthood: Priestly. Of, relating to, or suggesting

sacerdotalism

↓

Sacerdotalism Religious belief emphasizing the powers of priests as essential mediators between God and man.

II. Is there a clergy in Christ’s church?

A. Are the Pastors/Overseers “clergy”?

  1. The N.T. authorizes Elders/Pastors, in each congregation

2. There was always a plurality, never just one man

a. “…Ordained them elders in every church” (Acts 14:23)

b. “…And ordain elders in every city…” (Titus 1:5)

c. “…let him call for the elders of the church…” (James 5:14)

d. “…the elders which are among you…” (1 Peter 5:1)

3. Elders are NOT a separate clergy

a. All of the definitions under clergy describe one who is an overseer

b. BUT, the New Testament does NOT elevate one overseer/elder above

others

(1 Peter 5:1-4)

c. Elders/Pastors in Christ’s church are NOT a “clergy” separate from other christians

C. Are there to be “mediators” or “priests” in the Lord’s church?

1. ALL Christians are priests

(1 Peter 2:5-10)

2. There is only ONE high priest

(Hebrews 4:14-16)

3. There is only ONE mediator

(1 Timothy 2:5-6)  (5)For there is one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus;  (6)  Who gave himself a ransom for all, to be testified in due time.

  1. Jesus is speaking on our behalf before the Father

i. (IF we are in subjection to Him)

b. NO man or woman can take Christ’s place as a mediator

c. The word “clergy” means “priest”, which is a mediator

d. Christians go directly to God through Christ. No other mediation is needed!

e. Therefore, NO ONE can be in submission to Jesus and claim to be “clergy”, or a mediator, between God and man.

NOTE: This is how the dictionary defines “clergy” and how the religious world, in general, understands the word.

III. CONCLUSION

A. ALL Christians are priests, or, “clergy”

1. NO mediator is needed between Christians and God except Jesus

B. A separate “Clergy” has no place in the church of Jesus Christ

1. The pastoral/shepherd duties are met by elders

a. God has authorized shepherds (elders), NOT “priests” or “mediators”

2. The “nature” of the clergy (a “go-between” to God) violates God’s instructions for His church.

3. A separate clergy, by definition, takes Christ’s place as mediator between God and man, and (implicitly) does away with the need for a savior.

(John 17;17, 12:48)

2. By examining ALL things religious that we practice

a. Even if “we’ve always done it that way”

(1 Thessalonians 5:21)  Prove all things; hold fast that which is good.
