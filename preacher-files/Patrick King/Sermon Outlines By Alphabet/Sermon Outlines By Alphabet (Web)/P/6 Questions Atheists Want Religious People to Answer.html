a.    Yes, but only the type of evolution that can be observed in nature, microevolution through changes in the DNA.

c.    What does the world tell us about evolution? Scientific method: 1) Make an observation, 2) Ask a question or state a purpose based on observation, 3) Do research and take notes, sometimes this includes or requires more observations, 4) Form or develop a hypothesis, 5) Design an experiment to test the hypothesis, 6) Form a conclusion [Scientific Method is not a ridged set of steps or rules; you may have to go back to any of the first 4 steps and rethink things before you move on from here.], 7) Report your results so that other scientists can repeat the test and see if they get repeatable results.     

                                         i.     Science begins with the Big Bang “hypothesis” [This word is a stretch, but we explain]. We discussed this idea in enough detail last time but without this and a whole lot of time (4 billion years), the evolution that is presented by scientists would not be possible.

                                      ii.     Hypothesis is a testable question or purpose for an experiment. Theory is a hypothesis that has been tested repeatedly with repeatable results.

                                    iii.     The evolution that scientists present is also just a hypothesis and not a theory [again a stretch based on the definition]. Science tells us that all life began with a sludge, they refer to as primordial ooze. After a surge of energy to the ooze, they say a strike of lightning, then randomly RNA came together to make amino acids and these amino acids came together to form proteins. This is how the first bacteria, known as Archaebacteria or ancient bacteria, still existing today first evolved. Darwin from a study of finches in the Galapagos Islands, presented the idea of what is now quoted as “survival of the fittest.” In his book, he describes this as meaning the species that is most fit in a particular environment. The overall theory he proposed here, he called “Natural Selection.” An example that he gave would be, [Fire in a wood with white and black moths.] Scientists today would tell us that these genes randomly appeared and that the white moth would had to have evolved into the black moth, based on how they interpret Natural Selection and survival of the fittest. The white moth was not fit and based on their definition should cease to exist. However, the white moth persists, why? Because in all the moths there was white DNA and black DNA. The moths expressing the black color are more likely to survive so those genes and the white genes will still be passed on. When the environment changes again, then the white moths will thrive and the black moth population will lower for a bit. In nature, there are no intermediate species or “the missing link” between two species because different species can’t even reproduce. For example, a horse and a donkey are two different species. One would say well they can make a mule, so they can reproduce. The problem is that the mule is naturally sterile meaning it cannot reproduce. All hybrid species like this are sterile. We can’t observe the type of evolution scientist teach in the world today. A bacteria evolving into a fish, a fish evolving into a dinosaur, dinosaurs into apes, and apes into people. The ironic thing is, that scientists today will say that we are in an evolutionary standstill. Meaning nothing is evolving today, even though they will try to push it in the textbooks and on documentaries. If it can’t be observed, then it can’t be tested.

V.            Do You Believe in Fate?

a.    Fate means the development of events beyond a person’s control, regarded as determined by a supernatural power. – (Oxford Languages definition)

                                         i.     The idea comes from Greek and Roman mythology with a group of three sisters called the Fates. They were goddesses that control the birth, life, and death of every person. Individuals could not have any control of their life, because the Fates were in control.

                                      ii.     The word “fate” is not found in the KJV of the Bible. Even the idea of fate is not found in the Bible. I do not believe in fate. However, I do believe in providence.

b.    Providence means the protective care of God or of nature as a spiritual power. – (Oxford Languages definition)

                                         i.     The word “providence” is only found once in the KJV in Acts 24:2 and it is referring to Felix, the governor over the Jews, as Tertullus was accusing Paul of being a disturber of the peace and profaning the temple.

                                      ii.     The Greek word for “providence” (pronoyah) means forethought, this is, provident care and supply: - providence, provision. -Strong’s Concordance

                                    iii.     Although, the only place in the KJV uses the word in reference to a man, the idea is taught all throughout the Bible. This is different from “fate”, because with fate, there is absolutely no control, but providence [through the bounds of nature and not supernaturally or miraculously] still leaves room for free will of the individual to accept God’s care and provision or to reject it.

c.    A great example of God’s providence is found in the book of Esther. Again, the word is not mentioned, but the idea or thought of God’s care and provision is seen throughout the book. Nothing was done supernaturally or miraculously, everyone was free to choose and make their own decisions.

                                         i.     I Cor. 10:1-33 [vs. 11-13]

1.    The Israelites rejected God’s providence and, in their case, miraculous care. They were able to see the miracles of God and his providence firsthand and still gave in to temptation and sinned against God.

2.     Paul is warning Christians that we too have a choice to obey or disobey God’s will when He provides us an escape from temptations, so we don’t sin against him.

3.     In other words, the individual has complete control over his/her life and, through obedience to God’s will, where he/she ends up on Judgment Day. (Providence, not fate.)
