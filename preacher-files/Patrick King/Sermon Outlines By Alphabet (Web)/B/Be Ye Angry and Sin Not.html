Scripture Reading: Ephesians 4:26— “Be ye angry, and sin not: let not the sun go down upon your wrath.” This message explores how believers can handle anger biblically without falling into sin.

I. The Reality of Anger

A. Anger is a God-given Emotion

  • God Himself expresses anger (Psalm 7:11 — “God judgeth the righteous, and God is angry with the wicked every day.”)
  • Jesus showed righteous anger (John 2:15 — when He cleansed the temple)

B. Not All Anger is Sinful

  • Ephesians 4:26 commands us to “Be ye angry” — implying anger in itself is not sin
  • It’s what we do with our anger that determines if we sin

II. The Risk of Sin in Anger

A. Anger Can Turn into Wrath

  • James 1:20 — “For the wrath of man worketh not the righteousness of God.”
  • Anger becomes sin when it is driven by pride, revenge, bitterness, or malice

B. Warning Against Lingering Anger

  • “Let not the sun go down upon your wrath” (Ephesians 4:26b)
  • Prolonged anger leads to bitterness (Hebrews 12:15)
  • Harboring wrath opens the door to the devil (Ephesians 4:27)

III. Righteous Ways to Handle Anger

A. Respond, Don’t React

  • Proverbs 15:1 — “A soft answer turneth away wrath: but grievous words stir up anger.”
  • Control your tongue; don't let emotions dictate your response (Proverbs 14:29)

B. Bring It to God in Prayer

  • Psalm 37:8 — “Cease from anger, and forsake wrath: fret not thyself in any wise to do evil.”
  • Vent vertically (to God) before venting horizontally (to others)

C. Seek Reconciliation

  • Matthew 5:23-24 — Be quick to resolve conflicts
  • Colossians 3:13 — “Forbearing one another, and forgiving one another...”

IV. The Example of Christ

A. Controlled Zeal

  • Mark 3:5 — Jesus looked around “with anger, being grieved for the hardness of their hearts”
  • His anger was always rooted in love and justice, never selfishness

B. Forgiveness at the Cross

  • Luke 23:34 — “Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do.”
  • Even when wronged, Jesus did not retaliate in anger

Conclusion:

Ephesians 4:31-32 (KJV) — “Let all bitterness, and wrath, and anger, and clamour, and evil speaking, be put away from you... And be ye kind one to another...”

  • Let God shape your heart so anger doesn’t control your actions
  • Be quick to forgive, slow to speak, and always seek peace

Application/Call to Action:

  • Examine your heart: Is your anger righteous or selfish?
  • Commit to dealing with offenses biblically and promptly
  • Ask the Holy Spirit for self-control and a heart of mercy
