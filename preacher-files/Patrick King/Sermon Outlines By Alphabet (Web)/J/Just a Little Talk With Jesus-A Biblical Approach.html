D. Chorus- “Just a Little Talk With Jesus” is a hymn that encourages people to have a conversation with Jesus. The line “Now when you feel a little prayer-wheel turning” is a metaphor for the Holy Spirit’s presence in one’s life. The phrase “prayer-wheel” is not a Buddhist reference, but rather a simple device used in some Christian communities, including evangelicals and Pentecostals, to a series of prayer practices that focus the mind and spirit of Christians and encourage them to “pray without ceasing” 12. The song suggests that when one feels the Holy Spirit’s presence, they should talk to Jesus, and it will make everything right 3.

E. Come Thou Fount of Every Blessing: “here I raise my Ebenezer”

      1. Ebenezer- stone of help

2. I Sam. 7:12- The Israelites won a battle against the Philistines and Samual took a stone and made a memorial to God and named it Ebenezer meaning stone of help because God helped them win the battle.

3. Some people have taken this a step too far and they will have a prayer rock they keep in their pocket or wear a cross around their neck and rub on it. These items help them to remember to “Pray without ceasing.” I Thess. 5:17.

4. No where is the scriptures are Christians commanded, suggested, or do we see an example of physical objects being used to help remember to obey any commandment our Lord has given. These are idols and should be avoided.

B. Emphasizing the importance of aligning our prayers and songs with God's Word

Conclusion:

A.  This is song is unscriptural in the following ways:

1.    It suggests the Holy Spirit is helping us and guiding us and this is not how the Holy Spirit operates today.

2.    It suggests that it is okay to have physical reminders for prayer and these simply become idols.

3.    It suggests that it is okay to have a casual conversation with Christ and that is not Christ’s role in prayer.

   B. The hymn’s intention is to bring a focus to prayer and that is important; however, everything we do should be done according to the authority of Christ and His Word.

   C. This includes always having biblical and sincere approach towards prayer and the songs that we sing.

   D. This is because our worship is to please God and not simply to make us feel good or entertain us.
