INTRODUCTION: Scripture Reading-Deuteronomy 8:1-5

·      Read Deuteronomy 8:6-20

·      There are places God leads us that we would never choose for ourselves.

o  Places that feel dry.

o  Places that feel lonely.

o  Places where the scenery doesn’t change and the prayers don’t seem to be answered as quickly as we hoped.

·      Scripture has a name for those seasons: the wilderness.

·      The wilderness is

o  not punishment.

o  not abandonment

o  not God forgetting you.

·      The wilderness is a classroom, a foreign land. Just like the Israelites God is still leading us:

o  Deuteronomy 8:2 “And thou shalt remember all the way which the LORD thy God led thee these forty years in the wilderness, to humble thee, and to prove thee, to know what was in thine heart, whether thou wouldest keep his commandments, or no.”

·      Israel spent 40 years in the wilderness — not because God was slow, but because God was shaping them. And the same God who led them leads us. The same God who taught them teaches us.

·      This morning, I want us to look at three lessons God teaches in the wilderness — lessons we cannot learn anywhere else.

LESSON 1 — The Wilderness Teaches Us to Depend on God

·      [Exd. 16:1-4] Israel had just come out of Egypt. They had seen miracles. They had walked through the Red Sea. They had watched God defeat Pharaoh.

·      But now they are hungry.

·      And hunger has a way of revealing what we really trust.

·      Exodus 16:3 says the people grumbled, “Would that we had died in Egypt.”

o  6 different times they wished they had died or never left Egypt:

§  Exd 14:11-12; Exd 16:3; Exd 17:3; Numb 14:2; Numb 20:5; Numb 21:5

o  Isn’t it amazing how quickly we make the past look better when the present gets uncomfortable?

·      But God responds with grace:
“I will rain down bread from heaven.”

o  Not a warehouse of bread.

o  Not a year’s supply.

o  Not even a week’s supply.

o  Just enough for today.

·      Why?
Because God wasn’t just feeding their stomachs — He was training their hearts.

·      Deuteronomy 8:3 says,
“He humbled you, causing you to hunger and then feeding you with manna… to teach you that man does not live by bread alone but by every word that comes from the mouth of the Lord.”

·      The wilderness strips away our illusions of self‑sufficiency.

o  It teaches us that God is not one of our resources — He is our Source.

o  Sometimes God lets the job dry up.

o  Sometimes God lets the plan fall apart.

o  Sometimes God lets the strength run out.

·      Not to destroy us — but to teach us to depend on Him.

LESSON 2 — The Wilderness Teaches Us to Trust God’s Timing

• [Numbers 9:15–23] In Numbers 9, God teaches Israel one of the hardest lessons of the wilderness: You don’t get to choose the timing — you only get to choose the trust.

• The cloud of God’s presence would rest over the tabernacle…

-       Sometimes one day.

-       Sometimes a few days.

-       Sometimes a month.

-       Sometimes a year.

• And Israel had one responsibility: “At the command of the Lord they camped, and at the command of the Lord they set out.” -ESV

• They never knew how long the cloud would stay. They never knew when it would move. They never knew when the season would change.

• And that’s exactly how God trains us.

• Have you ever been in a season where you kept asking in your heart: “God, why am I still here?” “Why hasn’t this changed yet?” “Why hasn’t the door opened?” “Why is this taking so long?”

• The wilderness exposes our impatience — but it also reveals God’s faithfulness.

• When the cloud stayed, they stayed. When the cloud moved, they moved. Not early. Not late. Not on their own schedule. On His.

• God’s timing is rarely predictable, but it is always perfect (flawless and at the fullness of His time).

• If God gave you everything the moment you asked for it, you would never learn to trust Him. You would trust the blessing — not the Blesser.

• Waiting is not wasted time. It is training time.

• In the wilderness, God is not just preparing the promise for you — He is preparing you for the promise, the promise for everyone, the ultimate promise that His son will return to take His own to that heavenly home.

LESSON 3 — The Wilderness Teaches Us Who God Really Is

·      Deuteronomy 8:5 says, “Thou shalt also consider in thine heart, that, as a man chasteneth his son, so the LORD thy God chasteneth thee.”

·      Chasten or discipline here doesn’t mean punishment.

o  It means formation.

o  It means shaping.

o  It means teaching.

·      Israel learned things in the wilderness they could not have learned in Egypt and would not have learned in Canaan.

·      They learned:

o   God is a Provider

o   God is a Protector

o   God is a Father

o   God is Faithful

o   God is Present

o   God is Enough

The wilderness is where God becomes real.

o   Not the God of your parents.

o   Not the God of the preacher.

o   Not the God of your childhood.

o   Your God.

The wilderness is where head knowledge becomes heart knowledge. The wilderness is where God shapes us through His Word if we are willing to learn from the lessons He provides us. Rom. 10:17 “So then faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.” II Peter 1, James 1:22, and Colossians 3:16 remind us that simply having knowledge of God is not enough. We have to understand His word and properly apply it in wisdom. We should want and we need a faith that is not dependent on what others believe and do but what I believe and do. Hebrews 11 gives us many examples of people who believed and did something to show that faith. They are examples to us that God takes care of those who are obedient to Him and His will.

CONCLUSION — The Wilderness Is Not the End

·      Israel didn’t stay in the wilderness. And neither will we.

·      The wilderness is a season — not a sentence.

·      God leads us through the wilderness, not into a dead end.

·      And when we come out — and we will — we will come out with:

o  A deeper faith

o  A stronger trust

o  A clearer identity

o  A closer walk with God

·      The wilderness is not where God abandons us. It’s where He prepares us.

·      Through the Word…

o  God is teaching us.

o  God is shaping us.

o  God is leading us.

o  The question is, are we willing to study His word, understand it, and apply it in our lives?

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Lessons From the Wilderness — Jesus in the Wilderness

INTRODUCTION: Scripture Reading- Hebrews 4:14-16

·      This morning we looked at Israel’s wilderness — a place of testing, shaping, and dependence. Tonight, we look at Jesus’ wilderness — a place of victory, obedience, and identification.

·      Israel spent 40 years in the wilderness. Jesus spent 40 days.

·      Israel failed. Jesus overcame.

·      Israel doubted God’s Word. Jesus stood on God’s Word.

·      Israel questioned God’s presence. Jesus trusted God’s presence.

·      And here’s the good news: Jesus didn’t go into the wilderness to show off — He went to show us the way through.

·      The wilderness is not just a place where we learn about ourselves. It’s a place where we learn about our Savior.

·      Tonight, I want us to look at three lessons Jesus teaches us in the wilderness — lessons that transform how we face temptation, trials, and spiritual battles.

LESSON 1 — Jesus Shows Us That Temptation Is Not a Sign of Weakness (Matthew 4:1)

·      Matthew 4:1

·      Notice:

o  Jesus didn’t wander into the wilderness.

o  He didn’t stumble into it.

o  He didn’t get lost.

o  He was led.

·      If the Spirit led Jesus into the wilderness, then the wilderness cannot be a place of shame.

·      Temptation is not a sign that you’re far from God. Temptation is often a sign that you’re right where God wants you — in a place where your faith is being strengthened.

o  James 1:2-4 (2) My brethren, count it all joy when ye fall into divers temptations; (3) Knowing this, that the trying of your faith worketh patience. (4) But let patience have her perfect work, that ye may be perfect and entire, wanting nothing.

·      Jesus was tempted:

o  When He was hungry

o  When He was tired

o  When He was alone

·      The enemy loves to strike when we feel empty.

·      But Jesus shows us that temptation is not sin. Temptation is the battleground where obedience is proven.

·      You are not unspiritual because you’re tempted. You are not weak because you’re tempted. You are not failing because you’re tempted.

·      Jesus was tempted — and He was without sin.

LESSON 2 — Jesus Shows Us How to Fight With the Word (Matthew 4:2-11)

·      Every time Satan tempted Jesus, Jesus responded the same way:

·      “It is written.”

o  Not:

§  “I feel…”

§  “I think…”

§  “I believe…”

§  “In my opinion…”

§  But “It is written.”

·      Jesus didn’t argue with the devil.

o  He didn’t negotiate with the devil.

o  He didn’t entertain the devil.

·      He answered with Scripture. Why? Because the Word of God is not just information — it is a weapon.

o  Ephesians 6 calls it “the sword of the Spirit.”

§  In all of the armor listed in Ephesians 6, it is the Christian’s only offensive weapon.

o  When the enemy attacked Jesus’ identity —
“If You are the Son of God…”
— Jesus fought back with the Word.

o  When the enemy attacked Jesus’ trust —
“Throw Yourself down…”
— Jesus fought back with the Word.

o  When the enemy attacked Jesus’ worship —
“Bow down to me…”
— Jesus fought back with the Word.

·      Jesus teaches us that victory in the wilderness is not about willpower — it’s about Word‑power.

·      You don’t overcome temptation by trying harder to say no or to shake thoughts out of your head. You overcome by standing on what God has said, believing it, trusting it, and obeying it.

·      Notice in vs. 11 what happened after this exchange of words with Satan. Satan left (James 4:7 “Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you.”) and after Satan leaves, Jesus is cared for by the angels. When we overcome and we have our victory in Heaven, we too will be cared for and not have to worry about temptation ever again.

LESSON 3 — Jesus Shows Us That We Never Face the Wilderness Alone (Hebrews 4:14-16)

·      [Hebrews 4:14-16] Hebrews 4:15 says, “We do not have a high priest who is unable to sympathize with our weaknesses, but one who has been tempted in every way, yet without sin.”

·      Jesus didn’t go into the wilderness for Himself. He went for us.

·      He went so He could say:

o  “I know what it feels like to be hungry.”

o  “I know what it feels like to be lonely.”

o  “I know what it feels like to be tempted.”

o  “I know what it feels like to be attacked.”

o  “I know what it feels like to be in a dry place.”

o  And because He knows, He can help.

·      Hebrews 4:16 says, “Let us then approach the throne of grace with confidence…”

o  Why confidence?

o  Because the One sitting on the throne has walked through the wilderness Himself.

·      Jesus is not a distant Savior. He is a present Helper.

o  He doesn’t just save us from sin — He strengthens us in our struggles.

o  He doesn’t just redeem our souls — He walks with us in our wilderness. That is of course when we are willing to study and meditate on the Word and apply it in wisdom, allowing it to be our guide.

CONCLUSION — Jesus Turns the Wilderness Into a Place of Victory

·       The wilderness didn’t defeat Jesus. Jesus defeated it.

·       And because He overcame, we can overcome.

·       Because He stood firm, we can stand firm.

·       Because He won the battle, we can win the battle.

·       The wilderness is not where you lose. The wilderness is where God proves Himself faithful.

·       And when we come out — and we will — we will come out stronger, wiser, and more anchored in Christ.

·       So tonight, if you’re in a wilderness:

o   Jesus understands

o   Jesus is with you

o   Jesus fights for you

o   Jesus strengthens you

o   Jesus leads you through

o   We just have to let Him.

·       If you’re waiting on miracle, you’ll be waiting a long time. It’s not a miracle that will lead us out but God, His Word, and our willingness to work and follow Him.
