Introduction: Scripture Reading- Judges 16:6-11

Samson, a tragic hero of Scripture.

Gifted physically, but ensnared by pagan culture.

Ethical weakness despite great strength.

Scripture Reference: Judges chapters 13-16.

Discussion:

I. Samson’s Prophecy and Birth (Chapter 13:1-25)

Angel’s announcement to Samson’s parents.

Nazirite vow: No wine, uncut hair, and no contact with dead bodies.

Purpose: To begin deliverance from the Philistines.

II. Samson’s Strength and Downfall (Chapter 16a vs. 1-22)

Physical feats: Lion, foxes, and 1,000 men with a jawbone.

Chapter 15: Battles the Philistines over the death of his wife and father-in-law (after he had put her away for a problem they had in Chapter 14) and becomes Judge over Israel. [Read 15:4-7]

Fatal attraction: Delilah’s betrayal.

Loss of strength: Hair cut, eyes gouged out.

Spiritual blindness: Ignoring God’s warnings.

I Cor. 10:12-13 “Wherefore let him that thinketh he standeth take heed lest he fall. There hath no temptation taken you but such as is common to man: but God is faithful, who will not suffer you to be tempted above that ye are able; but will with the temptation also make a way to escape, that ye may be able to bear it.”

III. Samson’s Redemption (Chapter 16b vs. 23-31)

Prayer for strength: In captivity, Samson turns to God.

Final act: Toppling the Philistine temple.

Symbolic death: Samson’s sacrifice for Israel.

IV. Lessons from Samson’s Life

God’s grace: Despite Samson’s flaws, God used him.

I Tim. 1:12-16 [Read]

Consequences of compromise: Samson’s moral failings. Led him to his capture and ultimately to his death.

Repentance and restoration: Samson’s final prayer. He killed more Philistines in death than he ever did in life.

Application: Our own strengths and weaknesses.

James 1:1-18 Our strengths come from God and our weaknesses come from our own mistakes (giving in to temptation).

Conclusion:

Samson’s legacy: A mix of triumph and tragedy.

God’s faithfulness: Even when we stumble.

Challenge: Seek God’s strength and avoid moral compromise. May the historic account of Samson inspire us to rely on God’s grace and avoid the pitfalls of compromise with sin.

Author: Patrick King
