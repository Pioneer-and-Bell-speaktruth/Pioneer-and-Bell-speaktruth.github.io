Series ^N6 Rejoice Always Joy Through Trials (Autorecovered)

Introduction: Scripture Reading- I Thessalonians 5:16-18

  • Life is filled with trials—sickness, sorrow, loss, and difficulty. Yet, the Christian is called to maintain a spirit of joy. This joy is not dependent on our circumstances but is rooted in our relationship with Jesus Christ. Paul’s exhortation to the Thessalonians challenges us to develop a perspective that transcends hardship.
  • Paul knew hardship- 2 Cor. 11:23-28; 30

I. Rejoice Evermore [pantoteh- at all times] (v.16)

  • Philippians 4:4 – 'Rejoice in the Lord alway: and again I say, Rejoice.'
  • Joy is a command, not just an emotion. It is possible because our joy is in the Lord, not in the fleeting pleasures or pains of life. Romans 14:17 tells us, 'For the kingdom of God is not meat and drink; but righteousness, and peace, and joy in the Holy Ghost.' Even when life is hard, we can rejoice because we know who holds our future.

II. Pray Without Ceasing (v.17)

  • Romans 12:12 – 'Rejoicing in hope; patient in tribulation; continuing instant in prayer;'
  • Constant prayer is the lifeline that keeps us connected to the heart of God. It allows us to bring our burdens before the throne of grace. In Philippians 4:6-7, Paul writes, 'Be careful for nothing; but in every thing by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known unto God. And the peace of God, which passeth all understanding, shall keep your hearts and minds through Christ Jesus.' This peace enables us to rejoice, even in trouble.

III. In Every Thing Give Thanks (v.18)

  • Ephesians 5:20 – 'Giving thanks always [pantoteh- at all times] for [hooper- super, above, superior to] all things [all circumstances] unto God and the Father in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ;'
  • Gratitude transforms our trials. When we give thanks in everything—not necessarily for everything—we acknowledge that God is at work. James 1:2-4 reminds us, 'My brethren, count it all joy when ye fall into divers temptations; Knowing this, that the trying of your faith worketh patience. But let patience have her perfect work, that ye may be perfect and entire, wanting nothing.' Romans 8:28 assures us that God works all things together for good to them that love Him.

Conclusion

  • Joy in trials may seem unnatural, but the scriptures tell us it's possible. It is the product of a life rooted in Christ. True happiness is not the absence of suffering, but the presence of God in the midst of our suffering. Romans 5:3-5 teaches us that tribulations produce patience, experience, and hope—an unashamed hope because of the love of God [I Peter 3:15-16]. Let us rejoice always, pray continually, and give thanks in everything.
  • 2 Cor. 12:7-10
