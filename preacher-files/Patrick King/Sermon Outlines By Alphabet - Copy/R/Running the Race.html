Introduction: Scripture Reading- 1 Cor. 9:24-27

About the context: 1 Corinthians 9:14-23 [Read] vs. 1-13 Paul defends his apostleship towards the Gentiles and especially for those at Corinth and he is also making an argument for preachers being paid for the work they do in the Lord.

B. vs. 22-23 Commentator “He would not sin against God to save the soul of his neighbour, but he would very cheerfully and readily deny himself. The rights of God he could not give up, but he might resign his own, and he very often did so for the good of others.”

C. The Christian life is a race and Paul teaches how to run the race not just for us to win but how to help others to cross that finish line as well, because we’re not running this race alone.

Discussion:

I. Finish What You Start

A. vs. 24a “…they which run in a race run all,”

  1. Isaiah 40:28-31 [Read] faint- to tire, to fly (turn and flee)
  1. II Cor. 4:1 reminds us we have a service and we are not to faint or quit
  1. Gal. 6:9 reminds us to continue in well doing first to the household of faith, Christians, and then to those that are without and faint not in this responsibility
  1. Heb. 12:5 Exhorts us not to faint when we are chastised and rebuked by God

B. Have the mindset, beginning to end, that you are the “ONE” to win the race. Vs. 24b “…but one receieveth the prize? So run, that ye may obtain.”

  1. Paul is not saying that only one person is going to make it to Heaven. He is making an analogy and comparing an earthly race to our Christian service. A runner will give everything he has in him to win first place. We as Christians should give everything we have to make it to Heaven or obtain our prize.
  1. A runner is not going to start a race with the mindset of “I’m not going to win this race. I don’t even have a chance.” Otherwise, why should they even show up to the race in the first place.
    1. I Thess. 5:9-11 wrath-punishment, appointed-purposed
      1. God Expects Us to Exercise Self-Control and Work for the Prize

vs. 25a (ASV 1901) - " And every man that striveth in the games exerciseth self-control in all things.” Striveth-struggle

All athletes, no matter what sport they are participating in, have to clear their mind of everything going on around them. As Christians, we to have to clear our mind of the distractions of the world around us to focus on God and his commands and desires for us. We have to keep the world out in order to keep our mind pure.

Eccl. 12:9-14 [Read] He doesn’t say to not study, he says to know when to stop.

Self-discipline in the Christian race

When we obey the gospel, we are saying we are old enough to make a decision about knowing right and wrong and accepting accountability for our sins in our life up to that point.

This means we no longer need someone to tell us when to pray, we should have the self-discipline to pray throughout the day.

This means we no longer need someone to tell us to apologize or repent of a wrong that we of committed toward another person, we just face the music and go take care of the problem.

Self-disciple as Christians running the race, trying to make it to Heaven and obtain the prize of salvation, means that we obey God without someone telling us what to do every step of the way and holding our hand.

A disciplined runner doesn’t have someone wake them up in the morning, get them breakfast, push them out the door to go run or train for that 5k or marathon, no they just get up and do the work.

God expects the same from us.

Conclusion:

A. 7 years ago the Brownlee brothers, Jonny and Alister, were in a race. Alister was in front of his brother and close to the front of the race. He was about to collapse as Jonny, the oldest of the two, came running up behind him. Jonny grabbed Alister’s arm and put it over his shoulder and helped Alister run towards the finish line. As they approached the finish line, Jonny stayed back and allowed Alister move forward and collapse over the finish line in front of him. To us this might sound like a bad thing, but to runners, Jonny was allowing his brother Alister to finish in 2nd place in front of him and let him have all the glory and by helping him, Alister was able to receive medical attention sooner.

B. How does this relate to us? Paul is telling us in our text that we are running this race in life but not alone. We do need to make sure we are being obedient and focused but we also need to make sure we are not being selfish but rather helping each to the finish line. Alister had to stand on the 2nd place podium by himself, just like we will have to stand by ourselves before the judgment seat of Christ on Judgement Day but there’s nothing wrong with helping each other get there.
