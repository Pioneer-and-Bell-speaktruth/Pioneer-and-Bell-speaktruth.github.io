  12. “Christianity is Not a Religion of Certainty”:
    1. While many things spoken of in the Bible have been proven to be true by scientists: 1) the life is in the blood, 2) the paths of the see, 3) the stars sing their own songs, 4) the earth is round, etc. There are yet many more things that cannot be proven or demonstrated by science, which make them miraculous or supernatural, beyond the realm of natural explanation.
    2. Examples: The virgin birth of Jesus, the resurrection of Jesus, the parting of the Red Sea (no matter how much they try to explain it away), the stopping of time, and so many more that Jesus and His apostles did in the first century. There were done to show the power of God and there were many witnesses to these events.
    3. Matt. 9:8; Matt. 14:14-21 (~5,000 men + women and children)

Heb. 11:1 does a really good job at defining faith. “Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen.” -KJV “Now faith is the assurance of things hoped for, a conviction of things not seen.” -ASV [substance- hoopostasis: support, concrete, assurance, substance; evidence- elengkhos: proof, conviction, evidence, reproof]

  14. “Christianity Can Be Used to Justify Harmful Behavior”:
    1. Notice the wording here, “Christianity has been used to justify…”, not Christianity justifies everything from slavery to genocide. Man can always say and do what they want, but it doesn’t every make it right or accurate. Especially when it comes to God and His Word. The NT speaks about literal earthly slavery, but it neither condones nor condemns it. It speaks of man being either of servant or slave to righteousness or to sin, but again this is not condoning or condemning slavery.
