Theme: The certainty of God’s judgment and the urgency of spiritual readiness.

Introduction: Scripture Reading- Hebrews 9:24-28

Briefly introduce the hymn “There’s a Great Day Coming.” (361)

Highlight its solemn tone and powerful message about the coming judgment.

Ask: Are we ready for that great day?

I. The Certainty of the Great Day

Text: Hebrews 9:27 – “It is appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment.”

  1. God has set a day of judgment (Acts 17:31).
  1. It will be a day of reckoning for all people (Romans 14:10-12).

C. It is not a myth or metaphor—it is a divine appointment.

Illustration: Just as we prepare for important earthly appointments, how much more should we prepare for this eternal one?

II. The Dual Nature of the Great Day

Text: Matthew 25:31-46 – The separation of the sheep and the goats.

  1. A day of joy for the faithful.

Eternal reward, reunion with Christ.

“A day of rejoicing” for those who are ready.

  1. A day of sorrow for the unprepared. (2 Pet. 3:10)

Eternal separation, regret, and judgment.

“A day of weeping” for those who rejected grace.

Quote from the hymn: “There’s a bright day coming… but its brightness shall only come to them that love the Lord.”

III. The Call to Readiness

Text: Matthew 24:44 – “Therefore be ye also ready: for in such an hour as ye think not the Son of man cometh.”

  1. Salvation is available now (2 Corinthians 6:2).
  1. We must live in a state of spiritual alertness.

C. Obedient Faith in Christ is the only way to be prepared (John 14:6).

Application: Are you living like today could be the day

Conclusion:

For obedient believers, the great day is not to be feared but anticipated.

We should live with hope, urgency, and faithfulness with full assurance that Christ will return someday and take His adopted brothers and sisters home with Him to the Father. (2 Pet. 3:14 “Wherefore, beloved, seeing that ye look for such things, be diligent that ye may be found of him in peace, without spot, and blameless.”)
